\section*{Abstract}
We ask whether a causal, normalized intraday variance-risk-premium (VRP) curve can time defined-risk NIFTY option structures after realistic execution and capital constraints. The foundation contains 43,018,677 one-minute option rows across 1,371 expected trading sessions, independently recomputed Black--Scholes implied volatility (IV), date-aware contract rules, India VIX/spot joins and a six-slot research SPAN join. A full unconditional coverage audit limits defensible fixed-contract research to ATM$\pm3$ and at most 180 minutes; the primary exit is 60 minutes. Provider IV is not accepted at face value, realized volatility (RV) uses only information available at entry, and IV/RV are converted to matched intraday variance before VRP is ranked within the intraday clock.

The original level/direction rule, 12 percentile-cross variants, structure/horizon grid, tail-reversal rules and a causal feature rescue all fail net of one-lot costs. A later, explicitly post-hoc upper-85 percentile upward crossing with a short iron fly, three causal deterioration gates and a frozen quality switch is historically positive: 86 executions from 132 signals, \INR153,013 gross, \INR44,029 costs and \INR108,983 net under a \INR10 lakh sizing experiment. The largest rupee drawdown is \INR7,982; the largest percentage drawdown is 0.762\% in a different episode. However, the composite-score later-period Spearman bootstrap interval crosses zero; 2024, post-20-Nov-2024 and event-week results are weaker; and the same archive influenced selection. The correct decision is therefore \textbf{reject standalone intraday VRP timing and retain one p85 candidate only for frozen forward shadow observation}.

\decisionbox{The p85 candidate is approved for this submission and a forward-shadow protocol, \emph{not} for live-capital deployment. Current live allocation is \INR0.}

\subsection*{1.2 Independent Black--Scholes inversion}
For call/put prices $C,P$, spot $S$, strike $K$, rate $r=10\%$, dividend yield $q=0$, and ACT/365 time $T$ to verified 15:30 IST expiry,
\[
d_1=\frac{\ln(S/K)+(r-q+\tfrac12\sigma^2)T}{\sigma\sqrt{T}},\quad d_2=d_1-\sigma\sqrt{T},\quad
C=Se^{-qT}N(d_1)-Ke^{-rT}N(d_2),\quad P=Ke^{-rT}N(-d_2)-Se^{-qT}N(-d_1).
\]
No-arbitrage bounds are checked before inversion. Newton is attempted first and Brent provides a bounded fallback: 28,196,221 and 5,085,391 successful rows respectively; only 48 solver failures remain. Repricing residuals are approximately $5.46\times10^{-12}$ at the median, $6.26\times10^{-9}$ at p95 and $1.05\times10^{-7}$ maximum. Provider IV is retained only as a comparison field.

\subsection*{1.3 Timestamp-aware SPAN treatment -- and its limit}
The research release joins BOD/ID1/ID2/ID3 reference slots and selects the latest matched slot not later than entry (for the original 895 H1 trades: BOD 298, ID1 473, ID2 119, ID3 5). This is timestamp-aware within the retained reference schedule and gives entry-specific scenario arrays. It is \textbf{not proof of historical archive arrival}: retained publication timestamps are unproven, and the strict point-in-time release yields no usable historical SPAN matches. Margin is therefore a conservative timestamp-scheduled reconstruction, not a claim of observed broker margin at that instant.

\subsection*{1.4 Dataset limitations carried into every conclusion}
\begin{itemize}
\item The source is rolling ATM$\pm10$ \texttt{WEEK} history, not a complete absolute-strike chain. Evidence supports a \emph{nearest-listed-expiry proxy}; it does not prove actual historical nearest-weekly identity in every row.
\item No historical bid/ask, queue position or expired-futures minute archive exists. Fills require a conservative model and longer paths are non-randomly censored as ATM migrates.
\item The versioned 751-row, three-date sample is for schema/reproducibility; full Parquets stay outside Git and are pinned by corpus SHA-256 \texttt{aee5db...252}.
\end{itemize}

\section*{2. Playable universe and volatility normalization}
\subsection*{2.1 Unconditional moneyness $\times$ horizon audit}
Every theoretical entry minute on every available session was audited at 15/30/60/90/120/180/240/300 minutes and offsets ATM$\pm0$ through ATM$\pm10$. Contract identity is frozen at entry; a later rolling label is never substituted. For the four-leg ATM$\pm3$ window, 60-minute exact endpoints cover 99.7766\% of 430,665 windows, strict minute paths cover 99.6754\%, and a last-observation-carried-forward rule capped at ten minutes reaches 99.8546\% with only 258 proxies (0.0599\%). At 180 minutes, 266,698 windows have 99.5208\% exact endpoints, 99.0926\% strict paths and 99.7068\% ten-minute-stale coverage; 621 proxies (0.2328\%) remain.

\subsection*{2.2 IV, RV and VRP are matched to the intraday clock}
The signal does not subtract an arbitrary daily RV statistic from a chain IV percentage, and it does not use the upstream gold spot-BSM IV at face value. Synchronized ATM$-1$/ATM/ATM$+1$ calls and puts imply a parity forward,
\[
F_t=\operatorname{median}_{K}\left\{K+e^{rT}[C_t(K)-P_t(K)]\right\},
\]
then CE and PE IV are independently inverted with discounted Black--76 and averaged at ATM. Local ATM$\pm3$ CE/PE IV supplies skew and curvature; provider IV is reconciliation-only. The ATM variance forecast is $V^{IV}_{t,H}=\widehat\sigma_{IV,t}^{\,2}\tau_H$, where $\tau_H=H/(365\cdot24\cdot60)$. RV is built causally from one-minute log returns observed up to $t$. On a matching interval,
\[
V^{RV}_{t,H}=\sum_{i=t-H+1}^{t}r_i^2,\qquad
\widehat\sigma_{RV,t,H}=\sqrt{V^{RV}_{t,H}/\tau_H},\qquad
VRP_{t,H}=V^{IV}_{t,H}-V^{RV}_{t,H}.
\]
The implementation also retains annualized variance spread $\widehat\sigma_{IV}^2-\widehat\sigma_{RV}^2$, \(\log(IV/RV)\), IV--RV divergence and 5/15/30-minute changes. Matching conventions makes the magnitude economically interpretable; common positive scaling preserves crossing direction.

\subsection*{2.3 Causal curve level, direction and acceleration}
Intraday IV/RV are strongly seasonal. We therefore rank VRP against historical observations from the same minute-of-day, using only prior dates:
\[
q_t=\frac{1+\sum_{s<t,\,m(s)=m(t)}\mathbf{1}[VRP_s\le VRP_t]}{n_{t,m}+1},\quad
v_t=q_t-q_{t-5},\quad a_t=v_t-v_{t-5}.
\]
The hypothesis family uses zero crossings, percentile-tail crossings, $v_t$, $a_t$ and tail reversals. Signals execute on the next exact minute; exit is the same frozen contracts at $t+60$ (120/180 only as declared diagnostics). One signal per session avoids overlapping duplicates in the base tests.

\subsection*{2.4 Initial number crunching}
Unconditional matched 60-minute VRP is positive in 39.54\% of windows. Complete ATM/$-3$PE/$+3$CE surfaces exist in 511,859 windows (99.88\%). Raw rank dependence is weak: Spearman correlations of short-condor P\&L with VRP percentile, percentile velocity, percentile acceleration and raw-VRP acceleration are respectively 0.1381, 0.0147, $-0.0012$ and 0.0031. This motivates a falsifiable crossing test, but not a trading claim.

\subsection*{3.1 Why the condor barely moved while costs looked large}
At 6--20 DTE, 60 minutes is a short window for a four-leg, partially Greek-offset condor: wing and body moves cancel, net vega/theta are modest, and the signal is not a direct tradable on the scalar VRP. Yet entry plus exit require eight orders. Under H1, the average bill was \INR160 brokerage, \INR59.18 modeled slippage, \INR30.39 GST, \INR12.37 STT, \INR8.68 exchange charges and about \INR0.53 other regulatory charges. Thus small gross structure movement, not an accounting bug, was the central failure.

\subsection*{3.2 Structure/horizon and longer-hold diagnostics}
The cost-aware grid compared condors, iron flies, butterflies and two-leg verticals at 60/120/180 minutes: 48 cells, zero positive-net-mean cells. The best was a lower-10-down 180-minute bull call spread (219 trades, \INR139.16 gross, $-\INR19.22$ net). Some 180-minute gross means exceeded \INR65, consistent with a slower maturation story, but round-trip costs remained roughly \INR249--311. Multi-day testing produced only one cell with at least 80\% coverage and it failed. For the apparent top-reversal 180-minute subgroup, 96 of 191 signals are evaluable and \textbf{95 are explicitly unevaluated}; the missing half cannot be assumed random.

\subsection*{3.3 Final causal feature rescue before closing standalone VRP}
A feature model used IV/RV divergence, $\log(IV/RV)$, VRP level/slope/acceleration, local skew/curvature, spot returns, DTE, Greeks, credit/debit and cost hurdles. Its 2025--26 confirmation had 190 trades, gross $-\INR6,130$, net $-\INR31,355$, mean net $-\INR165.02$ and failed six of eight gates. Module 3 therefore closes the standalone VRP hypothesis family rather than mining another crossing.

\section*{4. H4: the one retained p85 regime-conditional candidate}
\subsection*{4.1 Frozen trade contract}
At the first causal upper-85 percentile crossing upward, enter next minute in a nearest-listed-expiry-proxy \textbf{short iron fly} with every leg inside ATM$\pm3$ at entry; close the same contracts after 60 minutes. The structure is fully defined-risk. Historical lot size and latest research SPAN slot not later than entry are used. India VIX informs the slippage regime when present; contemporaneous reconstructed ATM IV $\times100$ substitutes where absent.

Three discovery-period deterioration conditions reject entries:
\[
\Delta IV_{5m}\le -0.046817,\qquad \Delta IV_{15m}\le -0.114873,\qquad
\Delta \widehat\sigma_{RV,5m}\le -0.02651714.
\]
The intuition is structure-specific: a short fly entered while IV/RV are already collapsing has too little remaining premium response relative to eight-order friction. Thresholds were learned on 2021--23 and frozen for later diagnostics; they are not a pristine prospective discovery.

\subsection*{4.2 Confidence score, rank test and hard quality switch}
The causal score is 50\% gate cushion, 15\% inverse IV percentile, 15\% inverse RV percentile, 10\% inverse DTE percentile and 10\% entry-time score. Percentile maps are frozen from 2021--23. Spearman is computed against \emph{one-lot net P\&L before sizing}:
\[
\rho_s=\operatorname{corr}\big(\operatorname{rank}(score_t),\operatorname{rank}(PnL^{net}_{t,1lot})\big),
\]
which prevents an artificial correlation from assigning more lots to high scores. Full-sample $\rho_s=0.271$ (one-sided permutation $p=0.0014$); discovery $\rho_s=0.337$ with bootstrap CI $[0.100,0.551]$; combined 2024--26 $\rho_s=0.214$, one-sided $p=0.0472$, but bootstrap CI $[-0.066,0.484]$. Because the later lower bound is not positive, confidence-proportional sizing fails its strict validation gate. The final rule uses a simpler \textbf{hard switch}: execute only if score $>0.40$; power zero means score does not scale lots after admission.

\subsection*{4.3 Cost model and corrected quantity impact}
For integer lots $L$,
\[
PnL^{net}_t(L)=L\,PnL^{gross}_{t,1}-C^{Groww}_t(L)-C^{slip}_t(L).
\]
Groww charges include date-aware sell-side STT, brokerage, exchange transaction charge, SEBI fee, stamp, IPFT and GST. Selected entries have at least 6.08 DTE and exit after 60 minutes, so expiry-settlement STT, expiry-day ELM and disappearing calendar-spread benefit are outside the realized trade path rather than omitted applicable costs. Synthetic adverse slippage begins with premium/VIX, depth, staleness and observed volume/OI terms. The removed 10\%-per-extra-lot ladder was demonstrably dominant despite sub-1\% participation. Its corrected incremental factor is
\[
\frac{impact}{base}=\frac{L-1}{59}+sqrt{\frac{q_{inc}}{V_{1m}}}+\sqrt{\frac{q_{inc}}{OI}},
\]
so the smooth heuristic contributes one base-slippage unit around 60 lots while participation retains the liquidity penalty. At six lots, the original model's added impact was 94.7\% arbitrary ladder; the correction restores market variables as the main scaling logic. This is calibrated conservatism, not measured order-book impact.

\subsection*{4.4 Why H4 clears where the original condor does not}
Across 86 admitted signals at one equal lot, gross P\&L is \INR25,148, charges \INR17,270, slippage \INR3,401 and net \INR4,477: \INR292.42 gross, \INR240.36 total cost and \INR52.06 net per trade. The cushion is narrow at one lot. With exact historical profile sizing, gross averages \INR1,779 and total costs \INR512 per executed trade, leaving \INR1,267 net; fixed brokerage is amortized while the corrected impact curve remains moderate at 6.5 average lots. This is the specific economically viable candidate -- not evidence that arbitrary VRP tails work.

\decisionbox{H4 is retained because gross clears modeled cost at one lot and by a wider amount under feasible sizing. It remains post-hoc and conditional; no claim is made that p85 is a universal optimum.}

\section*{5. \INR10 lakh sizing, risk management and tearsheet}
\subsection*{5.1 Sizing rule selected from a broad exploratory grid}
Starting equity is \INR10,00,000. For each admitted trade,
\[
L_t=\min\left(
\left\lfloor\frac{0.35E_t}{M_{t,1}}\right\rfloor,
\left\lfloor\frac{0.04E_t}{Loss^{defined}_{t,1}+Reserve^{q95}_{cost,1}}\right\rfloor,
76\right),
\]
with integer lots and exact quantity-aware cost recomputation. The rule caps entry SPAN at 35\% of current equity, defined max loss plus discovery q95 round-trip cost reserve at 4\%, and capacity at 76 lots. There is no fitted drawdown or losing-streak brake. The 17,640-policy grid produced 4,392 discovery-eligible rules; discovery-vs-holdout policy-net rank correlation was 0.649. The chosen configuration (margin 35\%, risk 4\%, score floor 0.40, score power 0) lies in a useful neighborhood, but \textbf{is not a proven efficient frontier}: objectives, intratrade MTM paths, SPAN expansion and truly independent data are insufficient to identify a global risk-return optimum.

\subsection*{7.4 Conclusion}
The central result is a disciplined rejection: normalized intraday VRP level, tails, direction, velocity, acceleration and reversal do not by themselves generate enough 60--180 minute defined-risk movement to cover execution. A p85 short-iron-fly regime candidate produces a meaningful historical gross-to-cost cushion after gating and feasible sizing, with broad positive calendar P\&L and controlled completed-trade drawdown. But its discovery is post-hoc, rank stability is not decisive and market/data boundaries are material. The strategy is therefore complete enough to submit, not complete enough to fund.
